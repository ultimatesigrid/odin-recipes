Bibimbap (Korean Rice With Mixed Vegetables)

Description

Bibimbap is a warm Korean rice bowl, which is topped with veggies, meat, and a fried egg. It is a quite filling meal, relatively spicy and also healthy.

Once you try it, you'll want to make it again and again.

Ingredients

  • English cucumber
  • fresh spinach
  • soy sauce
  • olive oil
  • carrots
  • garlic
  • red pepper flakes
  • beef top round steak
  • eggs
  • white rice
  • sesame oil
  • sesame seeds
  • gochujang

Steps

  1. Stir together cucumber pieces and gochujang paste in a bowl; set aside.
  2. Bring about 2 cups water to a boil in a large nonstick skillet and stir in spinach; cook until bright green and wilted, 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Drain spinach and squeeze out as much moisture as possible; set spinach(cut into thin strips) aside in a bowl and stir in soy sauce.
  4. Heat 1 teaspoon olive oil in a large nonstick skillet; cook and stir carrots(cut into matchstick pieces) until softened, about 3 minutes.
  5. Stir in garlic(minced) and cook just until fragrant, about 1 minute. Stir in cucumber(cut into matchsticks) mixture; sprinkle with red pepper flakes. Set carrot mixture aside in a bowl.
  6. Brown beef in a clean nonstick skillet over medium heat, about 5 minutes per side; set aside.
  7. Heat remaining 1 teaspoon olive oil in another nonstick skillet over medium-low heat. Fry eggs just on one side until yolks are runny, but whites are firm, 2 to 4 minutes.
  8. Divide cooked rice into 4 large serving bowls; top with spinach mixture, a few pieces of beef(thinly sliced), and cucumber mixture. Place 1 egg atop each serving. Drizzle each bowl with 1 teaspoon sesame oil, a sprinkle of sesame seeds, and a small amount of gochujang paste if desired.

Estonian style sauerkraut

Description

Estonian people love this traditional dish mostly in the cold winter time. It goes well with boiled potatoes, as do most Estonian dishes, lol. It is called mulgikapsad in Estonian.

Ingredients

  • 1kg of sauerkraut, fermented cabbage, hapukapsas
  • 1 cup of pearl barley
  • 0.5kg of pork belly
  • chopped onion(optional)
  • salt, pepper, sugar, and caraway seeds for seasoning

Steps

  1. Put half the quantity of sauerkraut on to the bottom of a thick-bottomed pot and add washed grits and meat.
  2. Put remaining sauerkraut on top.
  3. Add water to cover ingredients and salt.
  4. Simmer on low heat. Check from time to time about water. Be sure that the bottom of the pot would have more than a few centimetres of water. If needed, add more water.
  5. When cabbage is a soft, season and adds some onion. Let set.
  6. Serve with boiled potatoes. Head isu!

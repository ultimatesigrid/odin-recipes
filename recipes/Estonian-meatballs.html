Estonian meatballs

Description

These Estonian meatballs are a really homey food for a lot of people who grew up in Estonia. They are most often made with a minced meat product that is pretty untypical outside the Baltic region. It is made with a mixed minced meat, mostly beef and pork. You can usually buy a 50:50 premade mix from the store. And off you go, making this humble dish.

This food is good with boiled potatoes and a white sauce. But it is also good, when it is cold and put on a rye bread.

Ingredients

  • 100ml dry breadcrumbs
  • 200ml liquid (water, cream, milk, sour cream)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp fine salt
  • 0.5 tsp freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Mix breadcrumbs and the chosen liquid in a mixing bowl and let stand for about five minutes.
  2. If you don't like the harshness of raw onion, then saut√© it gently in a bit of oil until translucent.
  3. Fold the (fried) onion, the meat, egg and seasoning into the breadcrumb mixture and stir until thoroughly combined.
  4. Using wet or oily hands, form the mixture into meatballs or patties. Fry in hot oil or batter on both sides for a few minutes, until golden brown and thoroughly cooked.
